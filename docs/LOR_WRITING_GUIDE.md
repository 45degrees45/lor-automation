# LOR Writing Guide — Team Lead Pointers for Employees

*Read this before reviewing any AI-generated draft. These are the 10 things every strong LOR must cover.*

---

## The Golden Rule

**Specific facts beat strong adjectives every time.**

A weak letter says: "He played a critical role and made invaluable contributions."
A strong letter says: "He made a significant contribution to this project by redesigning the inference pipeline, reducing model latency from 840ms to 95ms across 12 production services."

The AI generates the draft. Your job as reviewer is to ensure every paragraph has a specific fact behind it.

---

## ❌ Banned Phrases (flag these immediately in comments)

Never approve a draft that contains:
- "critical role" → replace with "significant contribution by [specific action]"
- "key role" → same
- "pivotal role" → same
- "instrumental" → same
- "invaluable contribution" → replace with the actual number/outcome
- "indispensable" → replace with the consequence of their absence
- "exceptional talent" → replace with the award, selection, or recognition that proves it

## The 10 Pillars — What to Check in Every Draft

### 1. Project Overview
**What to look for:** Reader should understand what the project is, what it does, and exactly where the petitioner sat within it — not just their title, but their function.

**Team lead comment if missing:**
> "Add one sentence explaining what [Project Name] actually does and what the petitioner's specific technical function was — not their job title."

---

### 2. Organizational Criticality
**What to look for:** Stakes. What would have broken if this project failed or didn't exist?

**Team lead comment if missing:**
> "Why did this organization need this project? What problem went unsolved before it? Add the business or technical consequence of this work not existing."

---

### 3. Significant Contribution
**What to look for:** The petitioner's personal, specific actions — not the team's.

**Correct phrasing:** "X made a significant contribution to this project by designing the [specific system] that [specific outcome]."

**Team lead comment if wrong:**
> "[USCIS FLAG] Replace 'played a critical role' with what he specifically did. Use: 'He made a significant contribution by...' followed by the exact technical action."

---

### 4. Challenges and Solutions
**What to look for:** What was technically hard? What approaches didn't work before? What did the petitioner try that was non-obvious?

**Team lead comment if missing:**
> "The challenge section is generic. What specifically failed before his approach? What made his solution non-obvious? Add the technical constraint that made this hard."

---

### 5. Success Metrics
**What to look for:** Real numbers. No adjectives.

✅ Good: "Reduced processing time by 73%, handling 2.4M requests/day"
❌ Bad: "Dramatically improved performance at massive scale"

**Team lead comment if weak:**
> "Replace all adjectives with numbers. What was the before/after? What is the scale? Ask the petitioner for the exact metrics — do not estimate."

---

### 6. Devastating Consequence
**What to look for:** What breaks if this person leaves tomorrow?

**Team lead comment if missing:**
> "Add one paragraph on what would be at risk if this person's contributions were removed. Which systems, products, or ongoing projects depend directly on their work?"

---

### 7. Distinction from Peers
**What to look for:** Awards, selections, invitations, recognitions — things that prove they are not average.

**Team lead comment if missing:**
> "What distinguishes this person from other engineers/researchers at their level? Add specific awards, conference invitations, review panel selections, or other recognitions that prove they are in the top tier of their field."

---

### 8. Organization-Wide Impact
**What to look for:** Did the work spread beyond their immediate team?

**Team lead comment if missing:**
> "How did this work affect the broader organization? Which other teams adopted it? Was it cited in leadership decisions or company strategy? Add at least one example of cross-team or company-wide impact."

---

### 9. Industry-Wide Impact
**What to look for:** Evidence that the work matters beyond this employer — open source, papers, standards, other companies using the approach.

**Team lead comment if missing:**
> "USCIS requires evidence that extraordinary ability is not limited to a single employer. Add the petitioner's open source contributions, published research, conference presentations, or instances where their approach was adopted by other organizations."

---

### 10. US Benefit
**What to look for:** Specific connection to US national interests — economy, healthcare, AI leadership, infrastructure, public good.

**Team lead comment if weak:**
> "The US benefit paragraph is too generic. Tie the work to a specific US priority — which US industry benefits? Which US companies or institutions depend on this type of work? What does the petitioner plan to build or research next in the US?"

---

## Approval Checklist

Before adding `[APPROVED]` comment to the Google Doc:

- [ ] No banned phrases anywhere in the letter
- [ ] All 10 pillars addressed with specific facts
- [ ] Every impact statement has a number
- [ ] Petitioner's personal contribution is clearly separated from team effort
- [ ] Industry-wide impact goes beyond the current employer
- [ ] US benefit is specific, not generic
